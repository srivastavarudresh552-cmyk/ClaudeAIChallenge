# Quarterly Growth Strategy Meeting Dashboard

A modern meeting-minutes dashboard that converts meeting transcripts into structured summaries, speaker insights, action items, risks, blockers, conflicts, and follow-up decisions.

## 📌 Overview

This project demonstrates a professional meeting intelligence interface designed for leadership teams and stakeholders. Instead of reading lengthy transcripts, users can quickly review key decisions, unresolved questions, ownership, and business risks through an organized dashboard.

The sample dashboard is based on a **Quarterly Growth Strategy Meeting** involving executive leadership and department heads.

## 📊 Dashboard Sections

### Summary

Provides a concise overview of meeting outcomes.

#### Example Metrics

| Metric | Value |
|----------|----------|
| Revenue Growth | 12% |
| Enterprise Deals Closed | 9 / 14 |
| Website Traffic Growth | +34% |
| Customer Retention | 94% |

---

### Speaker Summary

Summarizes each participant's contributions.

**Participants**

- CEO
- CFO
- CTO
- Head of Sales
- VP Marketing
- Product Director
- Customer Success Lead

---

### Action Items

Tracks follow-up tasks generated during the meeting.

| Task | Owner | Priority |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Complete architecture review | CTO | High |
| Resolve dashboard issues | Engineering Team | High |
| Finalize Q3 forecasts | Finance Team | Medium |
| Submit vendor proposals | CFO | Low |

---

### Open Questions

Captures unresolved topics requiring future discussion.

Examples:

- Will the analytics dashboard launch in August?
- Should the dashboard be announced at the conference?
- What will next year's hiring plan look like?
- Will the conference budget be approved?

---

### Risks & Blockers

Identifies factors that may impact project or business goals.

Examples:

- Revenue target risk due to delayed enterprise deals
- Engineering resource limitations
- Missing architecture review
- Dashboard performance concerns

---

### Conflicts

Highlights competing priorities discussed during the meeting.

Examples:

- Website redesign vs analytics dashboard delivery
- Hiring plan disagreements between departments
- Conference announcement vs uncertain release date
