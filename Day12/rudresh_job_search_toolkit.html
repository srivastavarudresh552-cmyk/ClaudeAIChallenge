Questions to prepare thoroughly

"Tell me about yourself."

Use your 30-second intro (Section 7) as the base. Expand with 1–2 project highlights and end with what you're looking for. Practice until it takes exactly 90 seconds.

"Why do you want to work here?"

Research the company's product, tech stack, and a recent blog post or launch before every interview. Mention one specific, concrete thing — never give a generic answer to this.

"What's your biggest weakness?"

Be honest and show active learning: "I'm still building my backend depth — particularly around system design and cloud infrastructure. I've started studying Next.js and I'm working through system design fundamentals in parallel with my coursework."

"Walk me through a technical challenge."

Use Zenith Banking's auth flow or the Webpack optimisation story. Structure as: the problem → your approach → what you tried that didn't work → the solution → what you'd do differently. Interviewers love the "what didn't work" part.

DSA / coding rounds

You're active on LeetCode, CodeChef, and Codeforces. For internship-level roles, focus on Arrays, Strings, Hashmaps, Two Pointers, and Recursion. Aim for 150+ LeetCode problems before major application cycles.
